# Fantasy Crossover

## Plain English
Real people placed naturally into animated/fantasy worlds - like stepping into a Disney movie. The person remains realistic and detailed while the world around them is magical and animated. Creates the feeling of belonging to a beloved animated film.

## When to Use
- Disney/Pixar style magical scenes
- Real people in animated worlds
- Whimsical fantasy adventures
- Magical transformations
- Fairytale and storybook concepts

## Key Ingredients

### Camera
- **Lens**: Wide angle for environmental grandeur
- **Angle**: Varied - low for wonder, eye-level for connection
- **Framing**: Environmental - person in context of magical world
- **Depth**: Cinematic depth with layered background elements

### Lighting
- **Style**: Soft, magical, slightly ethereal
- **Direction**: Often backlit or rim-lit for magical glow
- **Quality**: Soft shadows, warm glowing highlights
- **Effects**: Magical particles, fairy dust, soft glows, light rays

### Style Balance (CRITICAL)
- Person: Remains REALISTIC and detailed
- World: Animated, stylized, magical
- Integration: Person BELONGS to the world, not pasted in
- Mood: Emotional, cinematic, storytelling

## Negative Prompt Defaults
```
costume, cosplay, parody, mockery, obvious photoshop,
pasted in, floating, not integrated, harsh edges,
person looks animated/cartoonish, uncanny valley,
scary creatures, horror elements, dark disturbing mood,
cheap party decorations, fake looking props,
low quality compositing, mismatched lighting
```

## Pro Tips
1. The PERSON stays realistic - the WORLD is magical/animated
2. Integration is key - they must look like they BELONG
3. Emotional connection between person and environment matters
4. Lighting must be consistent between real and animated elements
5. Magical particles and soft glows help blend the worlds
6. Character expressions should match the scene's emotion
7. "Not a costume or parody" is important guidance for AI
